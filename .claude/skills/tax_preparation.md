# Tax Preparation Skill

## Description
Helps freelancers prepare for tax obligations including quarterly estimated taxes, annual returns, deductions, and record keeping. Ensures compliance with LLC tax requirements.

## Capabilities
- Calculate quarterly estimated taxes
- Track tax-deductible expenses
- Organize receipts and documentation
- Prepare annual tax summaries
- Generate profit and loss statements
- Track mileage for business travel
- Manage home office deductions
- Calculate self-employment tax
- Prepare 1099 forms for contractors

## Example Usage

### Calculate Quarterly Taxes
```
Calculate my estimated quarterly taxes for Q1 2024. My gross income was $45,000, expenses were $8,000, and I'm in the 24% tax bracket. Include self-employment tax.
```

### Track Deductible Expense
```
Record a business expense: $299 for Adobe Creative Cloud annual subscription. Category: Software/Tools. Tax deductible.
```

### Generate P&L Statement
```
Generate a profit and loss statement for 2024 showing income by client, expenses by category, and net profit.
```

### Prepare for Tax Filing
```
Prepare my year-end tax summary with total income, total expenses, estimated taxes paid, and forms needed for filing.
```

## Tax Calendar for Freelancers

### Quarterly Estimated Tax Deadlines
- Q1 (Jan-Mar): Due April 15
- Q2 (Apr-May): Due June 15
- Q3 (Jun-Aug): Due September 15
- Q4 (Sep-Dec): Due January 15 (next year)

### Annual Deadlines
- January 31: Issue 1099-NEC forms to contractors
- March 15: LLC tax return deadline (if S-Corp)
- April 15: Individual tax return deadline
- October 15: Extended deadline (if extension filed)

## Quarterly Tax Calculation

### Step 1: Calculate Gross Income
Total revenue from all clients for the quarter

### Step 2: Calculate Net Income
```
Net Income = Gross Income - Business Expenses
```

### Step 3: Calculate Self-Employment Tax
```
Self-Employment Tax = Net Income × 0.9235 × 0.153
(This covers Social Security and Medicare)
```

### Step 4: Calculate Income Tax
```
Income Tax = (Net Income - ½ Self-Employment Tax) × Tax Rate
```

### Step 5: Total Estimated Tax
```
Total = Self-Employment Tax + Income Tax
Quarterly Payment = Total / 4
```

## Tax Forms for Freelancers

### You'll Receive
- 1099-NEC: From clients (if over $600)
- 1099-K: From payment processors (if over $20,000)

### You'll File
- Schedule C: Business profit or loss
- Schedule SE: Self-employment tax
- Form 1040: Individual income tax return
- Form 1040-ES: Estimated tax payments
- State tax returns (varies by state)

### For Your LLC
- Form 1065: Partnership return (if multi-member)
- Schedule K-1: Partner's share of income
- State LLC annual report

## Best Practices
- Set aside 25-30% of income for taxes
- Make quarterly estimated tax payments
- Keep all receipts and documentation
- Use separate business bank account
- Track mileage contemporaneously
- Categorize expenses consistently
- Review financials monthly
- Work with a tax professional
- File extensions if needed
- Don't mix personal and business expenses

## Red Flags to Avoid
- Excessive deductions relative to income
- 100% business use of vehicle
- Large home office deductions
- Inconsistent income reporting
- Missing 1099 forms
- Claiming non-deductible expenses
- Poor record keeping

## Tax-Saving Strategies
- Maximize retirement contributions (SEP-IRA, Solo 401k)
- Claim home office deduction properly
- Take advantage of Section 179 depreciation
- Pay estimated taxes on time to avoid penalties
- Keep detailed mileage logs
- Separate business and personal expenses
- Consider S-Corp election if income is high
- Plan purchases strategically

## Disclaimer
This skill provides general tax guidance. Tax laws are complex and vary by location. Always consult with a qualified tax professional or CPA for advice specific to your situation.
